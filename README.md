## ok, the human version

I'm Lachy. 18, from the Mornington Peninsula: beaches, wineries, retirees, and at least one teenager running a multi-tenant SaaS out of his bedroom. I finished a Cert IV in IT and then went fully self-directed, which is a polite way of saying I learn by shipping things slightly beyond what I currently know how to build, repeatedly, until the gap closes.

on paper I'm a web developer. I run **Lachy's Web Dev** building sites for clients, and I sell website templates on Etsy. those are the side quests, and they pay for the servers. the main quest is AI engineering: agents, orchestration, the unglamorous plumbing between a model and the real world. that's where I'm locked in.

## the pantheon

here's the thing about being a solo founder at 18: you cannot hire anyone. so I recruited gods instead. it started as a naming scheme and turned into a worldview, because the oldest stories humans have are all about information: who sees everything, who hears everything, who holds the keys. the Greeks specced out the internet about three thousand years before electricity. I just implement.

### 👁️ Argus
*the hundred-eyed watcher. never all of his eyes asleep at once.*

you've met him. self-OSINT footprint scanner: 28 platforms, async enumeration, Gemini-grounded web intelligence, one clean standalone HTML report. open source, because a tool like this should be auditable by the people pointing it at themselves.

### 📣 Pheme
*goddess of rumour and news. started as a whisper, arrived everywhere.*

a self-hosted AI news scheduler CLI (TypeScript, Node, Gemini). you pick the topics, you set the cron schedule, and she delivers grounded daily digests to your terminal, a file, or your inbox. no feed, no algorithm auctioning your attention. just the news you actually asked for, on time, from a minor deity.

### 🗝️ Janus
*Roman god of doorways, gates and keys. two faces, so he reads both sides of every ticket.*

Janus lives inside **AID Helpdesk**, the most ambitious thing I run: a multi-tenant SaaS where IT admins manage Active Directory entirely from a browser. no VPN. no open inbound ports. a Windows agent polls outbound over HTTPS, so it works from behind any NAT on earth, school firewalls included. Janus (Claude Haiku) resolves tickets in plain English: password resets, account unlocks, bulk student OU moves. built for schools and SMBs. yes, I hired a literal god of doors to do password resets. he has never once complained.

### 🏛️ the AI Command Center
*no god name on this one. this is just Olympus.*

a local Node.js server plus a Discord bot, tunneled out through Cloudflare, that lets me command my whole fleet of Claude Code agents from anywhere, including my phone. it reads from my Obsidian vault, so every agent shares one long-term memory across sessions. mission control for everything above, and the place where the night shift clocks in.

## 🌙 the night shift

most nights I write the briefs, queue the agents, and go to bed. they don't. I wake up to fresh commits, a decision log, and sometimes a fix for a bug I hadn't even found yet. the first time it happened I just sat there staring at the diff like... bro. I was asleep for this.

I know how the sentence sounds: an 18 year old whose team works while he sleeps, and whose team is not people. it's either thrilling or unsettling, and I genuinely think working out which one is now part of the job. which is why:

## 📜 essays, not changelogs

the part of this I can't stop thinking about isn't the code, it's what the code implies. so I've started writing proper essays about it. research-paper energy, not dev-blog energy:

- **watch yourself.** on digital footprints, and why the only comfortable scan is the one you run on your own name. (from building Argus)
- **the loss function.** on optimisation, and what humans and models are both doing when they try to close the gap between what they are and what they're aiming at. (drafted. rewriting it until it stops being embarrassing.)
- **delegation without sleep.** on what authorship, trust and review actually mean when your collaborator works while you're unconscious. (from the night shift)
- **why the gods keep fitting.** Argus, Pheme, Janus: seeing, hearing, access. the oldest myths are about information asymmetry, which is why they map so cleanly onto software in 2026. (brewing)

they'll be published here when they're ready. I'm saying that publicly so I can't quietly not do it.
